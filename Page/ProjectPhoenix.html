Project Phoenix

The Premise

In the near future, military scientists are incarcerated in an underwater prison following a global peace declaration. When a glitch reverts your guard-bot creations back to their combat state, survival becomes a matter of stealth and precision. You play as Wagner, navigating the dark abyss to restore power to an escape elevator.

The "E-4" Unit: A lethal military bot moving with erratic, glitched patterns.

Technical Role: Lead Programmer

On a 3-month student project, I served as the Lead Programmer, maintaining technical stability and core gameplay systems. My key contributions include:

  • Core Gameplay Mechanics: Programmed the stun gun (limited to 2 charges), battery management, and flashlight mechanics.
  • Systems Engineering: Developed the environmental interaction systems for doors and a custom spawn/respawn framework.
  • Audio Implementation: Balanced and integrated spatialized 3D audio to create a high-tension, claustrophobic soundscape.

Technical Hurdle: Dynamic UI & Animation

I designed a high-polish Main Menu featuring animated backgrounds and "glitched" text effects. This posed a significant technical challenge within Unreal Engine's UMG system:

  • The Problem: Standard UI widgets do not natively support render materials for complex glitch effects.
  • The Solution: I converted a sequence into a custom sprite sheet and created a UI Material that functioned as a high-performance "cheap flipbook."
  • Result: This allowed for fluid, aesthetic animations that matched the game's theme without compromising performance.

Production Constraints

Developed in Unreal Engine 5.4. Despite team downsizing mid-production, we successfully created all original assets and custom meshes, ensuring a unique visual identity and a polished stealth-horror experience.
