The Premise

Bat Cave Defender is a fast-paced 2D arcade-style game designed to test player reflexes. As the sole guardian of the cave, you must fend off waves of insects approaching from three directions. Success depends on precision timing and the ability to distinguish between lethal threats and life-saving hearts.

Technical Role: Lead Programmer

This project focused on high-speed input handling and state-based enemy management. My primary technical contributions include:

  • Directional Attack Logic: Engineered a 3-way input system (Left, Right, Up) that requires frame-perfect timing to clear approaching threats.
  • Dynamic Difficulty Scaling: Programmed a difficulty curve that scales enemy speed and spawn rates based on the player's survival time.
  • Strategic Healing System: Implemented a "non-violent interaction" mechanic where players must selectively withhold attacks to allow healing items to reach the bat.

Gameplay Features

  • Reflex-Based Action: High-stakes 2D defense where enemies accelerate as the session progresses.
  • State Management: Enemies approach from randomized lanes (top and sides), requiring constant situational awareness.
  • Survival Loop: A high-replayability loop centered on beating personal best scores through mastery of the healing mechanic.
